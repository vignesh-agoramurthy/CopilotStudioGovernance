# 🚨 Final Mission: What will you make now? {#final-mission}

<mission-meta />

## 🎯 Mission Brief {#mission-brief}

Congratulations, Recruit. You’ve completed your training. Now it’s time to think about what comes next.

You now understand how to:
- Ground an agent in real knowledge  
- Shape how it responds  
- Add structure with topics, triggers, and actions  
- Test and refine something that behaves in a human way  

You now have all the foundational knowledge you need to build agents. The next step is to make this real by **building something that helps you.**

---

## Where to look next

The best ideas are usually hiding in your day-to-day work.

Start here:

- What questions do you answer repeatedly?  
- What information do you have to go and find every time?  
- What emails or messages do you rewrite again and again?  
- What documents do you read just to extract a few key points?  

If something takes time, happens often, and follows a pattern — it’s a strong candidate.

---

## Prompts to help you find your first real use case

Use these as thinking prompts to brainstorm ideas.

### 1. Knowledge and answers
- *What’s a topic where I regularly need to look things up in SharePoint or documents?*  
- *If I had an agent, what would I want it to answer instantly for me?*

### 2. Repetitive communication
- *What types of messages or emails do I write frequently?*  
- *What information do I already know that could be turned into a consistent draft response?*

### 3. Summarising and extracting
- *Which documents do I open just to find a few key details?*  
- *What would I want extracted and presented clearly in one response?*

### 4. Personal productivity
- *What tasks slow me down but don’t require deep judgement?*  
- *Where could an agent do the first pass and let me review?*

---

## A simple way to shape an idea

If you’re not sure where to start, define your idea in one pass:

- **Who is the user?** (usually you)  
- **What are they trying to do?**  
- **Where does the information come from?**  
- **What should the agent produce?** (answer, summary, draft, checklist)  

Keep it simple. If you can clearly answer these four questions, you’re ready to build.

---

## What good looks like

Your next agent doesn’t need to be complex.

A good first real-world agent:
- Is grounded in a small, well-known set of documents or sources  
- Produces something useful in seconds (answer, draft, summary)  
- Saves you time immediately  
- Still leaves you in control of the final outcome  

---

## Keep going

You don’t need a perfect idea or a full solution. Start by picking something small, building it, testing it, then continuing to iterate and refine. 
This is how agent development works.
